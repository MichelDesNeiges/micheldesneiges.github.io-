A New Voice for Environmental Law in New Brunswick

The Creation of the New Brunswick Environmental Law Society

Publication: Elements Magazine
Date: March 2007
News coverage archive

By Jean-Paul Bourque

A new chapter in environmental advocacy in New Brunswick began in February 2007 with the launch of the New Brunswick Environmental Law Society, a public-service organization dedicated to advancing environmental law in the province.

The organization was created by a group of New Brunswick environmentalists who have previously been involved in founding several successful environmental initiatives in the province. The initiative is led by Michel LeBlanc DesNeiges, a long-time environmental advocate in the region.

“In a way, this represents the culmination of a personal journey for me.”

The Society will function primarily as a think tank while developing initiatives that encourage greater public participation in the legal process.

Led by Mr. DesNeiges, the Society intends to build a strong provincial membership base and host a range of events and activities. Its first public initiative will be a major environmental law conference in March 2007, organized in collaboration with the Université de Moncton’s student environmental law society. The keynote speaker will be Canadian Supreme Court Justice Michel Bastarache.

Over the past fifteen years, Mr. DesNeiges has been involved in a series of pioneering environmental initiatives. A graduate of the Université de Moncton, he holds degrees in psychology and environmental studies. He has founded or helped launch several environmental organizations, including Écoversité, the Green Media Foundation, the Petitcodiac Riverkeeper, and the Hann Baykeeper initiative in Senegal.

Through the Green Media Foundation, Mr. DesNeiges has also contributed to several environmental communication and planning initiatives, including the drafting of the Town of Bouctouche’s green plan, the production of an Internet video series, and the television program Blue Green.

According to Mr. DesNeiges, “The Society will distinguish itself in the field of environmental law through its mission, which is to contribute to a better understanding of environmental law for the benefit of environmental organizations, the general public, and other non-legal institutions. The Society also has a specific mandate to support the student environmental law society at the Université de Moncton.”

Université de Moncton law students have recently created a student wing of the Society. The group’s first official initiative was the submission of a policy paper to the university’s academic authorities recommending the creation of a Centre for Sustainable Development Law at the university, as well as a public-service environmental law clinic.

Mr. DesNeiges envisions the clinic initially focusing on areas such as First Nations and Indigenous law, environmental mediation, and municipal and land-use law.

“The idea of using the law to advance environmental issues is still relatively new in New Brunswick. We are trying to build a network capable of responding to the legal needs of a wide range of community actors, including environmental non-governmental organizations,” says Mr. DesNeiges.

Somewhat ironically, one of the Society’s objectives is to avoid confrontation in legal matters whenever possible.

“By favouring tools such as environmental mediation, we hope to bring people together and resolve disputes without going to court. But we must also be prepared to respond to more adversarial environmental conflicts when necessary,” he adds.

Membership in the Society is open not only to lawyers but also to non-lawyers: citizens, First Nations community members, university scientists and professors, college and public-school teachers, other professionals, and representatives from municipal governments.

In the long term, the Society hopes to strengthen environmental governance in New Brunswick by building bridges between the legal community, academic institutions, environmental organizations, and the public.

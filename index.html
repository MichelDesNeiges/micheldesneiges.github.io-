Personal and Professional Experience

About: Michel DesNeiges (also known professionally as Michel L. DesNeiges and Michel LeBlanc DesNeiges) is a lawyer based in Moncton, New Brunswick, Canada. After a full career in the arena of public law, he now maintains a limited and selective legal practice, focusing on advisory work, targeted advocacy, and files of particular legal or public interest.

Legal career: Michel began his legal career as director and lead counsel for the Société pour l'avancement du droit de l'environnement du Nouveau-Brunswick (2007–2014). Beginning in 2008, his private practice expanded into several additional areas of public law, including criminal law, administrative law, and child protection law.

Environmental Advocacy: In the 1990s, as a university student, Michel led Écoversité, an environmental advocacy group, and produced radio and television programs to raise public awareness. In 1999, he spearheaded the creation of a Riverkeeper program that contributed to the restoration of the Petitcodiac River.

Teaching & Clinics: Beginning in 2007, he directed a public interest environmental law clinic and lectured at universities in New Brunswick, sharing insights on environmental law, criminal law, and advocacy.

Athletics: Goaltender on championship hockey teams at the high school level (Mathieu-Martin Matadors), junior level (Moncton Jr. Hawks, Atlantic Junior Canadians), and college level. National champion with the Université de Moncton Aigles Bleus (1989–90).

Martial Arts: Began training in the early 2000s; earned a black belt with Maritime Shinkyokushin Karate. Trained with leading karate masters in Canada and Japan. Over 15 years experience as a budō karate instructor.

Adventure Travel: Over a 25-year period, Michel traveled extensively across Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and the Americas, later expanding his travels into Asia and the Himalayas.

Selected Writings: Essays - A Few Musings About Martial Arts and The Law Essay (English) · May 30, 2010 A three-part reflective essay on how Kyokushin karate and legal training intersect through discipline, ritual, philosophy, and the pursuit of balance; Travel Writing - In Search of Complexity Essay (English translation) · March 5, 2001 Originally written in French; a travel essay on moments of simplicity and the deeper complexity found in movement, uncertainty, and sociopolitical reality in Central America and beyond; The Highs and Lows of Cuba Essay (English translation) · March 24, 2001 Originally written in French; a month in Cuba rendered through contradiction—hospitality and austerity, propaganda and conviction—ending with a small gesture of human warmth.
